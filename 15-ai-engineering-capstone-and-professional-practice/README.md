# Lesson 15: AI Engineering Capstone & Professional Practice

This lesson consolidates the entire curriculum into a capstone delivery framework and professional practice toolkit. It focuses on scoping, execution, teamwork, stakeholder communication, and final presentation/reflection.

## Sub-lessons

1. `15.1 Capstone Design & Scoping`
2. `15.2 Execution: Data, Modeling, Deployment & Documentation`
3. `15.3 Teamwork, Communication & Stakeholder Management`
4. `15.4 Final Presentation, Reflection & Next Steps`

## How to Use This Lesson

1. Start with design and scoping before building.
2. Use execution checklists to run your capstone end-to-end.
3. Apply communication playbooks for team and stakeholder alignment.
4. Close with a presentation and reflection plan that converts work into portfolio and interview assets.

## Bridge from Lesson 14
**Why this follows:** After surveying frontier directions, this lesson focuses on execution discipline: turning knowledge into a coherent, deliverable capstone.

**You should already know:** core AI engineering stacks, operational patterns, safety/governance controls, and domain specialization options.

**What you will do next:** scope, build, communicate, and present an end-to-end AI system as a portfolio-quality artifact.

## After This Lesson
Use your capstone and supporting artifacts as:
- portfolio projects for applications/interviews,
- launch-ready prototypes for product exploration,
- foundations for deeper research or specialization tracks.

## End-to-End Capstone Flow
Use this lesson as one integrated workflow, not four isolated chapters:

1. `15.1` Project selection and scoping.
2. `15.2` Architecture, implementation, evaluation, deployment, and documentation.
3. `15.3` Team/stakeholder execution and communication.
4. `15.4` Final presentation, reflection, and next-step roadmap.

## Curriculum Skill Demonstration Map
A strong final capstone should explicitly show evidence from earlier lessons:

- `Lessons 1-3`: clean code, testing discipline, classical baselines, robust evaluation.
- `Lessons 4-5`: deep learning and GenAI design choices.
- `Lessons 6 & 12`: MLOps/LLMOps deployment and observability.
- `Lessons 7 & 13`: agent orchestration plus safety/guardrail design.
- `Lessons 8 & 11`: responsible AI, product framing, and business/research communication.
- `Lessons 9-10 & 14`: specialization depth and technical judgment under constraints.

## Capstone Scoring Rubric (Quick View)
Use this high-level rubric before final submission:

- `20%` Problem framing and scope control.
- `25%` Technical architecture and implementation quality.
- `20%` Evaluation rigor and reliability evidence.
- `15%` Deployment/operations readiness.
- `10%` Documentation and reproducibility.
- `10%` Presentation clarity and reflection depth.

## Interview Preparation Coverage
Each sub-lesson includes interview Q&A focused on:

- project scoping and trade-off decisions,
- execution and deployment reasoning,
- communication and stakeholder management,
- final presentation and reflection narratives.
